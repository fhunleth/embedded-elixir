Roundup of Embedded and IoT Presentations in March

 Posted on April 6, 2017  |  Frank Hunleth

March was quite a month for talks starting with ElixirDaze in Florida, going to Erlang and Elixir Factory in San Francisco, and ending at the Lviv Ruby Meetup in Ukraine.

Here’s a list of embedded Elixir and Erlang related ones that we found:

  • Get Cooking With Nerves - Garth Hitchens
  • Building Communities of Smart Things Takes Nerves - Jean-François Cloutier
  • Instrumenting the World with the BEAM - Christopher Cote
  • Wireless Embedded Erlang Applications with Grisp Hardware Boards and Toolchain - Peer Stritzinger
  • Building Devices with Elixir Erlang using Nerves - Justin Schneck
  • Empowering devices for IoT with Erlang and FPGA - Irina Guberman
  • Instrumenting and Testing High Performance Switching Fabric for IoT - Vladan Djakovic
  • Piecemeal Into Space Reliability Safety and Erlang Principles - Brian Troutwine
  • Concurrency and Strong Types for IoT - Carl Hewitt
  • Interactive Robotics with Elixir and Nerves - Arto Bendiken

Did we miss any? Let us know in the comments.
